About Survey Plane Finder

This project detects aircraft flying aerial survey patterns using live ADS-B data from adsb.lol.

Survey aircraft typically fly back-and-forth parallel lines at a consistent altitude — a pattern used for LiDAR mapping, aerial photography, pipeline inspection, and environmental surveys.

The detector watches for these patterns in real time and publishes detections to this map. The dashed orange rectangle shows the area currently being monitored.

Data updates approximately every 60 seconds. Use the date arrows to browse past detections.
